EVALUATION AND MANAGEMENT (E/M) SERVICES BILLING GUIDELINES
American Medical Association (AMA) — CPT 2023 Edition
Effective Date: January 1, 2023

===========================================================================
OVERVIEW
===========================================================================

This document summarizes the revised office/outpatient Evaluation and Management
(E/M) guidelines effective January 1, 2021 and carried forward through 2023.
These guidelines represent the most significant revision to E/M coding in 25 years.

===========================================================================
SECTION 1: LEVEL SELECTION — TWO PATHWAYS
===========================================================================

Physicians may choose EITHER of the following methods to select the E/M level:

PATHWAY A: MEDICAL DECISION MAKING (MDM)
Recommended primary pathway. Level is based on complexity of MDM.

PATHWAY B: TOTAL TIME ON DATE OF SERVICE
Total time includes face-to-face time AND non-face-to-face time on the day
of the encounter (e.g., reviewing records, ordering tests, coordination).

The level is determined by WHICHEVER pathway results in the higher level —
the physician chooses which pathway to document and bill.

===========================================================================
SECTION 2: MDM LEVELS (PATHWAY A)
===========================================================================

MDM is based on THREE elements; level is determined by meeting requirements
in AT LEAST TWO of the three elements:

2.1 NUMBER AND COMPLEXITY OF PROBLEMS ADDRESSED
- Minimal (Level 2): 1 self-limited or minor problem
- Low (Level 3): 2+ self-limited/minor problems; OR 1 stable chronic illness;
                 OR 1 acute, uncomplicated illness/injury
- Moderate (Level 4): 1+ chronic illness with exacerbation/progression; OR 2+
                      stable chronic illnesses; OR new problem with uncertain prognosis;
                      OR undiagnosed new problem with uncertain prognosis;
                      OR acute illness with systemic symptoms
- High (Level 5): 1+ chronic illness with severe exacerbation; OR acute or chronic
                  illness/injury posing threat to life or bodily function

2.2 AMOUNT AND/OR COMPLEXITY OF DATA REVIEWED AND ANALYZED
- Minimal (Level 2): Results of order(s) reviewed/noted in record
- Low (Level 3): Review of external records OR ordering of tests/results
- Moderate (Level 4): Review of external records AND independent interpretation
                      of tests; OR discussion of management with external provider
- High (Level 5): Decision requiring independent interpretation of test performed
                  by another physician; OR decision requiring independent
                  historian; OR selection of management option requiring independent
                  interpretation with documented rationale

2.3 RISK OF COMPLICATIONS / MORBIDITY OR MORTALITY
- Minimal (Level 2): OTC drugs; minor surgical procedure without identified risk factors
- Low (Level 3): Prescription drug management; minor surgery with identified risk
                 factors; IV fluids without additives
- Moderate (Level 4): Prescription drug management with decision about drug change;
                      minor surgery with identified risk factors; IV drugs (other than
                      chemo); elective major surgery without identified risk
- High (Level 5): Drug therapy requiring intensive monitoring for toxicity; elective
                  major surgery with identified risk factors; emergency major surgery;
                  hospitalization; decision not to resuscitate

===========================================================================
SECTION 3: TIME-BASED BILLING (PATHWAY B) — 2023 THRESHOLDS
===========================================================================

Office/Outpatient E/M CPT Codes and Time Requirements:

CPT 99202 — New patient, straightforward MDM — 15–29 minutes
CPT 99203 — New patient, low MDM — 30–44 minutes
CPT 99204 — New patient, moderate MDM — 45–59 minutes
CPT 99205 — New patient, high MDM — 60–74 minutes

CPT 99211 — Established patient (may not require presence of physician) — no minimum
CPT 99212 — Established patient, straightforward MDM — 10–19 minutes
CPT 99213 — Established patient, low MDM — 20–29 minutes
CPT 99214 — Established patient, moderate MDM — 30–39 minutes
CPT 99215 — Established patient, high MDM — 40–54 minutes

===========================================================================
SECTION 4: HISTORY AND PHYSICAL EXAM
===========================================================================

IMPORTANT CHANGE FROM PRIOR GUIDELINES:
History and physical examination are NO LONGER used to determine E/M level.
Physicians must document a medically appropriate history and physical exam
but these elements are NOT part of the level-selection calculation.

===========================================================================
SECTION 5: DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS
===========================================================================

5.1 Required in All E/M Records
- Chief complaint or reason for visit
- Relevant history (medically appropriate to the visit)
- Findings of exam (medically appropriate to the visit)
- Date of service
- Complexity of MDM OR total time (whichever pathway used)
- Provider signature with credentials

5.2 For Time-Based Billing
- Total time on date of service must be documented
- A brief note explaining what was done during non-face-to-face time is advisable
  (reviewing records, ordering tests, care coordination, etc.)

5.3 Prohibited Practices
- Copy-and-paste ("cloning") of prior notes without attestation of review
  and any updates is considered fraudulent documentation
- Upcoding based on a single, brief MDM element that does not meet the 2-of-3
  threshold is an audit target

===========================================================================
SECTION 6: PROLONGED SERVICES — 2023
===========================================================================

CPT 99417 — Prolonged office or other outpatient E/M service (new or established
patient), each 15 minutes beyond the maximum time for Level 5 E/M service.
Bill in addition to 99205 or 99215.

Maximum time thresholds before 99417 applies:
- New patient (99205): after 74 minutes → bill 99417 for each additional 15 min
- Established patient (99215): after 54 minutes → bill 99417 for each additional 15 min
