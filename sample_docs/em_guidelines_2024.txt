EVALUATION AND MANAGEMENT (E/M) SERVICES BILLING GUIDELINES
Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) — MLN006764
Effective Date: January 1, 2024
Publication Date: November 2023

===========================================================================
OVERVIEW — 2024 UPDATES
===========================================================================

This document summarizes CMS updates to E/M billing effective January 1, 2024.
Several important changes have been made relative to 2023 AMA guidelines,
including new time thresholds, an updated prolonged services code, and
clarifications to MDM requirements.

===========================================================================
SECTION 1: LEVEL SELECTION — NO CHANGE IN PATHWAY STRUCTURE
===========================================================================

The two-pathway approach (MDM or Total Time) continues in 2024.
Physicians select the pathway that results in the appropriate level.

No changes to pathway eligibility or selection rules in 2024.

===========================================================================
SECTION 2: MDM LEVELS — 2024 CLARIFICATIONS
===========================================================================

2.1 NUMBER AND COMPLEXITY OF PROBLEMS ADDRESSED — CLARIFIED

NEW for 2024: "Acute illness with systemic symptoms" at Moderate MDM now
explicitly includes:
- COVID-19 post-acute sequelae (Long COVID) as a qualifying condition
- Behavioral health conditions requiring a change in treatment plan,
  when the change involves prescription medication

Previously, behavioral health medication changes required a separate code.
This alignment simplifies billing for integrated care visits.

2.2 DATA — SOCIAL DETERMINANTS OF HEALTH (SDOH) ADDED

NEW for 2024: Review and documentation of Social Determinants of Health
(SDOH) — including housing instability, food insecurity, or transportation
barriers — now counts as a data element under "review of external records
and/or notes" for Moderate MDM level.

This change aligns with CMS's health equity priorities.

2.3 RISK — NO CHANGES IN 2024

Risk categories and examples remain unchanged from the 2021/2023 guidelines.

===========================================================================
SECTION 3: TIME-BASED BILLING — 2024 REVISED THRESHOLDS
===========================================================================

IMPORTANT CHANGE: CMS has revised time thresholds for established patient codes
to better reflect actual physician work. New patient codes are unchanged.

Office/Outpatient E/M CPT Codes — 2024 Time Requirements:

NEW PATIENT (unchanged from 2023):
CPT 99202 — New patient, straightforward MDM — 15–29 minutes
CPT 99203 — New patient, low MDM — 30–44 minutes
CPT 99204 — New patient, moderate MDM — 45–59 minutes
CPT 99205 — New patient, high MDM — 60–74 minutes

ESTABLISHED PATIENT (REVISED 2024):
CPT 99211 — Established patient (no minimum — no change)
CPT 99212 — Established patient, straightforward MDM — 10–19 minutes (unchanged)
CPT 99213 — Established patient, low MDM — 20–29 minutes (unchanged)
CPT 99214 — Established patient, moderate MDM — 30–44 minutes [CHANGED: was 30–39 min]
CPT 99215 — Established patient, high MDM — 45–59 minutes [CHANGED: was 40–54 min]

Note: The increased time thresholds for 99214 and 99215 reflect updated
physician work relative value unit (RVU) recalibration by CMS.

===========================================================================
SECTION 4: HISTORY AND PHYSICAL EXAM — NO CHANGE
===========================================================================

History and physical exam continue to NOT determine E/M level.
Documentation of medically appropriate H&P remains required but is
not part of the level-selection criteria. No changes in 2024.

===========================================================================
SECTION 5: DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS — 2024 ADDITIONS
===========================================================================

5.1 No changes to core documentation requirements.

5.2 NEW for 2024 — SDOH Screening Documentation
If SDOH data is used as a MDM data element (see Section 2.2), the record
must include:
- The specific SDOH factor(s) identified
- How the SDOH information influenced clinical decision-making or referrals
- If a referral or resource connection was made, document the action taken

5.3 Audit Risk Areas Highlighted for 2024
- CMS Recovery Audit Contractors (RACs) have been directed to focus on
  99215 claims billed without documentation of high MDM for established patients
- Time documentation for 99214/99215 must reflect the new 2024 thresholds

===========================================================================
SECTION 6: PROLONGED SERVICES — 2024 CHANGES
===========================================================================

IMPORTANT CHANGE: CMS has replaced CPT 99417 with HCPCS G2212 for
Medicare patients only, effective January 1, 2024.

HCPCS G2212 — Prolonged office or other outpatient E/M service, each
15 minutes beyond the maximum time for Level 5 E/M service.
(Medicare billing only; non-Medicare payers may still use CPT 99417)

Time thresholds for prolonged service (REVISED for 2024):
- New patient (99205): 89+ minutes total before G2212 applies
  [CHANGED: was 75+ minutes in 2023]
- Established patient (99215): 69+ minutes total before G2212 applies
  [CHANGED: was 55+ minutes in 2023; reflects new 99215 max time]

Note: G2212 replaces 99417 ONLY on Medicare claims.
Commercial payers should continue using 99417 per AMA CPT guidance.

===========================================================================
SECTION 7: TELEHEALTH E/M — 2024 EXTENSION
===========================================================================

NEW: CMS has permanently extended the following telehealth flexibilities
for E/M services (previously temporary COVID-era waivers):

- Audio-only telehealth visits are permanently covered for established
  patients who do not have access to audio-video technology, with
  documentation of the limitation.
- Telehealth E/M visits for new patients are permanently allowed without
  the prior in-person requirement.
- Place of Service (POS) code 02 (Telehealth) applies; POS 10 (Home
  Telehealth) for visits where patient is in their home.
