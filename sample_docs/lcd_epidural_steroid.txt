COVERAGE INDICATIONS, LIMITATIONS, AND/OR MEDICAL NECESSITY
===========================================================================

I. INDICATIONS FOR COVERAGE

Epidural steroid injections (ESI) are considered medically necessary and covered
when ALL of the following criteria are met:

1. The patient has a documented diagnosis of:
   - Radiculopathy (cervical, thoracic, or lumbar) due to herniated intervertebral
     disc, spinal stenosis, spondylosis, or degenerative disc disease, OR
   - Spinal stenosis with neurogenic claudication

2. The patient has experienced moderate-to-severe pain (numeric rating scale >= 4/10)
   for a minimum of six (6) weeks despite conservative therapy.

3. Conservative therapy must include at least TWO of the following:
   - Physical therapy (minimum 4 sessions)
   - Anti-inflammatory medications (oral NSAIDs, oral corticosteroids)
   - Activity modification
   - Chiropractic manipulation

4. Clinical findings corroborate the pain generator:
   - MRI or CT imaging within the past 12 months documenting pathology consistent
     with the patient's symptoms
   - Physical examination findings consistent with radiculopathy or stenosis
     (e.g., positive straight leg raise, dermatomal sensory changes, motor weakness)

5. No contraindications to the procedure are present (see Section III, Exclusions).

III. LIMITATIONS AND EXCLUSIONS
===========================================================================

A. Frequency Limitations
   - No more than THREE (3) epidural steroid injections per spinal region per
     rolling 12-month period.
   - A minimum interval of TWO (2) weeks between injections in the same region.
   - No more than SIX (6) total epidural steroid injections across all spinal
     regions per rolling 12-month period.

B. Absolute Contraindications (Denials)
   The following conditions result in automatic denial of coverage:
   - Injection site infection or systemic infection
   - Bleeding diathesis or anticoagulation therapy that cannot be safely managed
     peri-procedurally
   - Patient refusal of imaging guidance (fluoroscopy or CT is required for
     transforaminal injections)
   - Arachnoiditis or prior complications from epidural steroid injections that
     represent a documented contraindication per treating physician

C. Relative Contraindications (Require Additional Documentation)
   - Diabetes mellitus: Blood glucose management plan must be documented
   - Immunocompromised patients: Infectious disease or primary physician clearance required
   - Pregnancy: Obstetric clearance required; epidural steroids not routinely covered
     in pregnant patients without documented exceptional clinical need

D. Non-Covered Services
   - Injections for primary diagnosis of axial back pain without radiculopathy or
     stenosis
   - Trigger point injections billed under epidural codes
   - Injections performed without a preceding conservative care trial (minimum 6 weeks)
   - Repeat injections when prior ESI in same region showed no clinical benefit
     (defined as <30% improvement in pain or function)

===========================================================================
IV. DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS
===========================================================================

The medical record must contain ALL of the following at the time of service:

1. Diagnosis and Indication
   - ICD-10 diagnosis code consistent with the covered indication
   - Written documentation of presenting symptoms, duration, and severity (pain score)

2. Conservative Therapy Record
   - Dates, types, and duration of conservative treatments tried and failed
   - Physical therapy records or notes from treating therapist (if applicable)

3. Imaging
   - MRI or CT report (within 12 months) with findings corroborating the diagnosis
   - Radiology report signed by a radiologist or interpreting physician

4. Procedure Note
   - Informed consent obtained and documented
   - Approach used (interlaminar vs. transforaminal)
   - Level(s) injected
   - Agent(s) and dose(s) administered
   - Imaging guidance used (type and documentation of real-time imaging)
   - Patient tolerance and any immediate complications

5. Outcomes Tracking (for repeat injections)
   - Documentation of clinical response to prior injection(s)
   - Pain scale scores before and after prior procedure(s)
   - Functional improvement or lack thereof

===========================================================================
VI. BILLING AND CODING NOTES
===========================================================================

- Fluoroscopy guidance (CPT 77003) or CT guidance (CPT 77012) must be billed
  separately when used; real-time imaging is required for transforaminal approaches.
- Bilateral injections at the same level on the same date are payable; append
  modifier -50 (Bilateral Procedure).
- Injections at multiple levels on the same date: bill the primary level first,
  then each additional level with the appropriate "add-on" code.
- Documentation must clearly state whether imaging guidance was used to support
  separate billing of guidance codes.
